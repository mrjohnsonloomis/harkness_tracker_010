Roles at the Table

  • Initiator — opens new lines of inquiry.
  • Connector — links ideas across speakers/texts.
  • Questioner — probes and clarifies assumptions.
  • Evidence Finder — grounds claims in the text.
  • Synthesizer — sums up and names emerging claims.
  • Devil’s Advocate — offers alternative readings.
  • Bridge Builder — includes quieter peers.
  • Close Reader — notices craft and diction.

Sentence Stems

  • “I noticed that …”
  • “Can we connect this to …?”
  • “What do you mean by …?”
  • “On page … the author writes …”
  • “So what I’m hearing is …”
  • “What if we considered … instead?”
  • “[Name], what’s your take?”
  • “The repetition here suggests …”

Live Norms

  • Share airtime and invite voices in.
  • Ground claims with textual evidence.
  • Listen first; respond to understand.
  • Disagree with ideas, not people.

Quick Reference (from the Guide)

Highlights adapted from your student guide for in-class use.

  • Before: read actively, prepare 2–3 questions, tag quotes with page #s.
  • During: take notes, balance talk/listen, build on peers.
  • After: reflect on your role(s) and plan a next move.
